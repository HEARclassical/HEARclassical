Welcome to HEAR Classical. The name HEAR stands for history, equality, and artistic representation. The goal of HEAR Classical is to create a platform for female composers and people of color to be promoted, performed, and celebrated. While this website is meant to be an informational source about composers, HEAR Classical also highlights projects that are being done to help communities through the intersection of music and activism.

Please explore our website and learn a little more about what we do, including our partnerships, ambassadors, concert series, and plans for community engagement.

Steven Nichols Banks

Steven Nichols Banks is a classical saxophonist, chamber musician, jazz and commercial saxophonist, music educator, advocate for diversity and inclusion in classical music, and composer. Steven serves as Assistant Professor of Saxophone and Jazz Studies at the Baldwin Wallace Conservatory of Music. He is the baritone saxophonist of the award-winning Kenari Quartet, and has recently started performing in the wind section of The Cleveland Orchestra. Steven is an advocate for diversity and inclusion in classical music education and performance. In March 2017, Steven gave a talk at the TEDxNorthwesternU 2017 conference with ideas about how to create change in institutionalized prejudices against women and people of color.

Steven has a Master of Music degree from the Northwestern University Bienen School of Music in Saxophone Performance, Bachelor of Arts degree in Saxophone Performance with a minor in Jazz Studies from the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music, and a high school diploma from the University of North Carolina School of the Arts.

Ariel Horowitz

Ariel Horowitz is a violinist, music educator, and social justice advocate currently pursuing her masters at the Yale School of Music. Ariel is the founder of The Heartbeat Project, a student-run program that provides music and math education for students living on the Navajo Reservation. The annual summer workshop combines the fundamentals of music and math for Navajo (Diné) K-12 students while cultivating a safe space for students to thrive and gain confidence in themselves. The Heartbeat Project strives to create an atmosphere of cultural exchange in which the impact of American colonialism on indigenous peoples is acknowledged, and the teaching artists respectfully engage with Diné music, cultures, and customs.
