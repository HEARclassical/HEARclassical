Welcome to HEAR Classical. HEAR stands for history, equality, and artistic representation. A unique platform that combines music and activism, HEAR is dedicated to promoting, performing, and celebrating music by female composers and people of color. In addition to the historical information collected on this site, HEAR Classical also highlights individual projects that advocate for these values in their communities.

Ariel Horowitz

Ariel Horowitz is a violinist, music educator, and social justice advocate currently pursuing her masters at the Yale School of Music. Ariel is the founder of The Heartbeat Project, a student-run program that provides music and math education for students living on the Navajo Reservation. The annual summer workshop combines the fundamentals of music and math for Navajo (DinÃ©) K-12 students while cultivating a safe space for students to thrive and gain confidence in themselves. The Heartbeat Project strives to create an atmosphere of cultural exchange in which the impact of American colonialism on indigenous peoples is acknowledged, and the teaching artists respectfully engage with DinÃ© music, cultures, and customs.

Chloe Swindler

Chloe Swindler is a classical and jazz trumpet soloist, chamber musician, music educator, and advocate for diversity and inclusion. Chloe studies trumpet at the Yale School of Music, and works for the Music in Schools Initiative, a program which gives teaching artists the opportunity to teach music to students in the greater New Haven area. Chloe is working on an archival project focusing on the history of African-American female instrumentalists from the late nineteenth century through today.
